Softening Commercial Insurance Rates and What It Means for Businesses

MI
MathsInsure
October 30, 2025
5 min read

After several years of hardening market conditions and consistent premium increases, the commercial insurance landscape is showing clear signs of softening in 2025. This shift presents significant opportunities for businesses to reduce insurance costs while maintaining or even enhancing their coverage levels.

The current softening cycle is characterized by increased competition among insurers, improved loss ratios, and abundant capacity in the marketplace. Understanding these trends and knowing how to leverage them can result in substantial savings for businesses across all industries.

Key Market Indicators Showing Softening Trends

-8.5% Average Commercial Premium Decrease
+22% Insurance Capacity Growth
15% Improved Insurer Loss Ratios
+18% New Market Entrants

What's Driving the Softening Market?

Several factors are contributing to the current softening in commercial insurance rates:

  • Increased Capitalization: Insurers have built strong capital reserves through years of profitable underwriting, creating capacity for more competitive pricing
  • Improved Loss Experience: Better risk management and fewer catastrophic losses have improved insurers' combined ratios
  • Technology Adoption: AI and data analytics have enabled more precise risk assessment and reduced operational costs
  • Market Competition: New insurtech entrants and expanded offerings from traditional carriers are increasing competition
  • Economic Factors: Stable economic conditions and lower-than-expected inflation have reduced claims severity

Coverage-Specific Rate Trends

Commercial Property

Trend: Decreasing 10-15%

Improved loss experience and better property risk modeling are driving significant premium reductions, especially for well-protected facilities.

General Liability

Trend: Decreasing 5-12%

Stable litigation environment and improved safety protocols are resulting in moderate premium decreases across most industries.

Workers' Compensation

Trend: Stable to Slight Decrease

Continued focus on workplace safety and return-to-work programs are maintaining stable rates with selective decreases for low-risk employers.

Cyber Liability

Trend: Increasing 5-20%

Rising cyber threats and regulatory requirements continue to drive premium increases, though at a slower pace than previous years.

Market Cycle Timeline: From Hard to Soft

2021-2023: Hard Market Peak

Sustained premium increases of 15-30% annually, restricted capacity, and tighter underwriting standards characterized this period of market hardening.

2024: Transition Phase

Early signs of softening emerged with rate increases slowing to 5-10% and increased competition for high-quality risks.

2025: Soft Market Established

Clear softening trend with widespread premium decreases, expanded capacity, and more flexible underwriting across most commercial lines.

2026 Forecast: Continued Softening

Expected continuation of soft market conditions with potential for further premium decreases and enhanced coverage terms.

"The current soft market represents a golden opportunity for businesses to strengthen their insurance programs while reducing costs. However, companies should avoid the temptation to underinsure simply because rates are lower. The key is to use these savings to enhance coverage where needed and build resilience for the next market cycle." - Maria Chen, Chief Underwriting Officer at Global Commercial Insurers

Negotiation Strategies for Maximum Savings

Leveraging Market Conditions in Your Favor

1
Request Multiple Quotes

Increased competition means insurers are more willing to compete on price and coverage terms. Obtain at least 3-5 competitive bids.

2
Bundle Coverages

Package policies often receive additional discounts as insurers seek to secure multiple lines of business from the same client.

3
Highlight Risk Improvements

Document safety improvements, loss prevention measures, and risk management initiatives to justify lower premiums.

4
Consider Higher Deductibles

With lower premiums, the savings from increased deductibles may provide better value than in previous hard market conditions.

Key Takeaways

  • The commercial insurance market is clearly softening in 2025, with average premium decreases of 8-15% across most coverage lines
  • Increased insurer capacity, improved loss experience, and technological advancements are driving the soft market conditions
  • Businesses should use this opportunity to enhance coverage, increase limits, and improve policy terms rather than simply reducing costs
  • Strategic negotiation and multiple quotes can maximize savings while maintaining comprehensive protection
  • The soft market cycle is expected to continue through 2026, providing an extended window for program optimization
  • Companies should avoid underinsuring during soft markets, as this creates vulnerability when the market eventually hardens again

Action Steps for Business Leaders

To capitalize on the current soft market conditions, business leaders should:

  • Conduct a Comprehensive Policy Review: Assess all current coverages and identify opportunities for enhancement
  • Initiate Early Renewal Discussions: Begin negotiations 90-120 days before policy expiration to allow time for market testing
  • Document Risk Management Achievements: Prepare evidence of safety improvements and loss reduction efforts
  • Evaluate Alternative Program Structures: Consider captive insurance, higher deductibles, or different coverage combinations
  • Plan for the Next Cycle: Use current savings to build reserves for future premium increases when the market hardens

The current softening in commercial insurance rates represents a significant opportunity for businesses to optimize their risk management programs. By taking a strategic approach and focusing on long-term protection rather than short-term savings, companies can emerge from this soft market cycle with stronger, more cost-effective insurance coverage.
